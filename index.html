Turtles

The sea's most mystical creatures

Different type of turtles include

  • Pond Slider

    • The pond slider
    is a species of turtle native to the southern and southeastern United States and northern Mexico. It has three subspecies, the best-known of which is the red-eared slider. (The other two subspecies are the yellow-bellied and Cumberland sliders.)

  • Alabama Red-Bellied Cooter

    • The Alabama red-bellied cooter
    is a member of the pond turtle family, Emydidae. It is found only in Alabama, USA, and is the official reptile of that state.

  • Florida Softshell Turtle

    • The Florida softshell turtle
    is the largest member of the softshell turtle family, Trionychidae, found in the Americas.

  • Malaysian Giant Turtle

    • With a carapace length of
    up to 80 cm / 2.62 ft. and a maximum weight of 50 kg / 110 lb., the Malaysian Giant Turtle is one of the largest turtle species in Southeast Asia. It is found in freshwater swamps, lakes and rivers in Peninsular Malaysia, and on the islands of Borneo and Sumatra.

  • Pig-Nosed Turtle

    • Found in the freshwater marshes and rivers of northern Australia,
    the pig-nosed turtle gets its name from its distinctive, pig-like snout, which is highly sensitive and helps the animal to forage for food.
